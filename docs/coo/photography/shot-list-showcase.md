# Shot List — Showcase Recipes (Full Stage Photography)

> 10 recipes × ~7 shots each = ~70 frames. These recipes carry full stage photography in the app.
> Every shot in this list is **must-have** unless marked *(optional)*.
> Work through shots in the order listed — it matches the cooking sequence.
> Keep this open on a tablet while shooting. Not on the phone you're shooting with.

---

## How to read this list

- **Type** — Mise en place / Stage / Action / Hero / Detail
- **Angle** — Overhead (90°, straight down) / 30° (one-arm above, tilted down) / Eye-level
- **Surface** — What the food or prop sits on
- **"Done" looks like** — The visual cue Patrick is shooting. This is the doneness signal, not a description.
- **Alt text** — Accessibility text for TalkBack users. Copy this directly into the recipe step's `photo_url` alt field.

## Recipe 3 — Spaghetti Carbonara
**Shoot weekend:** Weekend 3 (17–18 May 2026) — shoot alongside Bolognese
**Library status:** ✅ In library (`pasta-carbonara`)
**Surface:** Dark slate (prep stages), dark stoneware bowl (hero)
**Est. shoot time:** 1.5 hours (fast dish — prep takes longer than the cook)
**⚠️ Time-critical:** The carbonara emulsion window is approximately 60–90 seconds. Have the tripod locked and the shot framed *before* you take the pan off the heat.

| # | Type | Angle | Subject | "Done" looks like | Alt text |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Mise en place | Overhead | Guanciale or pancetta cubed, eggs + extra yolks in a bowl, finely grated pecorino Romano, freshly cracked black pepper | Guanciale cut uniformly. Eggs golden-yolk yellow visible in the bowl. Pecorino in a fine pile. Black pepper — coarse cracked, not pre-ground. | Cubed guanciale, a bowl of egg yolks, finely grated pecorino and coarsely cracked black pepper on a dark slate surface. |
| 2 | **Stage — KEY DONENESS CUE** | Overhead | Guanciale in the pan, fully rendered | Fat has rendered clear. The meat pieces are deep golden-amber and crisp on the edges, not pale or soft. A small pool of rendered fat in the pan. | A pan of rendered guanciale — deeply crisped amber cubes surrounded by a pool of clear rendered fat. |
| 3 | Stage | Overhead | The egg and cheese mixture in the bowl | Glossy, thick, uniform pale-yellow emulsion. Some pepper through it. This is what "ready to emulsify" looks like — not scrambled egg, not a thin liquid. | A bowl of egg yolks, pecorino and black pepper whisked to a thick, glossy pale-yellow emulsion. |
| 4 | **Stage — KEY DONENESS CUE (shoot fast)** | 30° | Pasta being tossed off the heat — sauce just formed | Sauce coats the pasta uniformly. The consistency is creamy, just thick enough to cling to strands, not so thick it clumps. Golden-orange colour. No white visible (means unincorporated egg). No solid curds (means scrambled). This is the single hardest shot in the whole season — be ready before the pan comes off the heat. | Spaghetti carbonara being tossed in a pan — each strand coated in a glossy, creamy golden-orange sauce. |
| 5 | **Hero** | 30° | Plated bowl — twirled spaghetti, guanciale on top, extra pecorino, coarse pepper | The pasta should have a shine to it. Guanciale pieces on top catching the light. Pepper visible as dark flecks. The bowl should be warm — plating on a cold bowl dulls the sauce within 30 seconds. | A dark stoneware bowl of spaghetti carbonara — glossy egg-yolk-coated pasta, crisped guanciale on top, pecorino dusting and coarse black pepper. |

**Notes:**
- Pre-frame shot 4 before you cook the pasta. Tripod position and angle locked. You will not have time to adjust after the pan comes off heat.
- Cook a second batch if shot 4 misses — carbonara takes 15 minutes from boiling pasta. Do not ship a "scrambled egg pasta" photo.
- The hero bowl must be a warm bowl. Place it in a low oven (100°C) for 10 minutes before plating.

**Notes:**

- The char marks on the chicken (shot 3) are critical. The pan must be genuinely screaming hot. If the pan isn't smoking before the chicken goes on, the marks will be pale and the photo will look underpowered.
