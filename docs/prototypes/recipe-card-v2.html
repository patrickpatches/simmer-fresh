Design rationale

What changed from the current card, what stayed, and why each decision was made.

Chef chip on the image, not below it

The chef attribution is moved onto the hero image (bottom-left, over the scrim) rather than appearing as a text line in the card body. This does two things: it frees vertical space in the body (the card doesn't grow taller), and it places attribution in visual dialogue with the food photo — the food and its author are one unit.

The chip uses a rust-filled pill on the image rather than the rust-tint-on-white treatment used in the detail page — because it needs legibility against any photo colour. Semi-transparent dark blur is the correct pattern here (same as iOS control centre chips).

At-a-glance strip — three facts only

The detail page shows five at-a-glance facts. The card is ~160px wide and can carry three comfortably. The three chosen are total time, difficulty, and cuisine — the three decision-making signals a user needs when scanning the grid: "how long?", "can I actually do this?", "what kind of food?".

If total_time_minutes is absent (current seed library), the whole strip is omitted. The card degrades cleanly to title + tagline, which is what users see today. No broken dashes, no "—" placeholders.

Display serif for the title

Georgia (the stand-in for Playfair Display) at 14px on a 160px card is readable and gives the card a cookbook quality that sans-serif titles don't. The title is the primary hierarchy — it gets the display font. Tagline gets sans italic, muted — a clear second tier.

Two-line clamp on the title prevents tall cards in the grid. If a recipe title is longer than two lines it truncates with ellipsis — users can read the full title on the detail page.

Planned vs favourited vs pantry-matched — three independent layers

The card supports three simultaneous overlay states, all designed to coexist without collision. Planned: sage circle with tick, top-right. Favourite: ghost heart icon (unfilled idle, filled when active), top-right (replaces planned if both — planned takes priority). Pantry match: dark pill "N of X", top-left.

These were all present in some form in the current card — the v2 pass tidies their placement and ensures they don't overlap the chef chip (bottom-left) or the "Photos soon" badge (bottom-right).

White surface card on sage-green background

The background is now tokens.bg (light sage #E8F0E6), not dark. Cards are white (tokens.surface). This matches the detail page's light context — the browse grid and the detail page feel like the same app, same moment.

The current app used a dark browse context. The Sage palette direction (confirmed earlier this session) inverted this. The card design here is the correct response to that palette change — white cards sit naturally on sage green, not on near-black.

Engineer handoff
Files to touch

mobile/src/components/RecipeCard.tsx — primary file. All visual changes live here.

mobile/app/(tabs)/index.tsx — only if card layout is currently inlined there rather than in RecipeCard.tsx.

New props / data access

At-a-glance strip requires: recipe.total_time_minutes, recipe.difficulty, recipe.categories.cuisines[0]. All three are optional — if any one is absent, omit the strip. Don't show a partial strip.

Chef chip requires: recipe.source?.chef. Conditionally rendered — omit if absent.

What does NOT change

Card tap → recipe detail navigation. Favourite toggle logic. Planned-recipe indicator logic. Pantry match badge logic. Card dimensions and grid layout. "Photos soon" badge logic. All of these already work — only the visual treatment changes, not the behaviour.

Tokens (all existing)

tokens.surface card bg · tokens.line border · tokens.primary source chip · tokens.sage planned badge ·tokens.ink title · tokens.muted tagline + strip. No new tokens required.
