Design notes

Why dark scrim on the pill badge rather than sky/blue?

Sky tokens work well inside the recipe detail screen on a white card background — they're clearly informational without being alarming. But on a recipe card photo (which can range from dark stew to pale salad), a light-blue pill would clash unpredictably. The dark scrim (rgba(26,19,14,0.62)) is colour-agnostic — it reads clearly against any photo, the same way the existing difficulty badge uses a white pill. The text colour matches the existing "EASY" badge approach.

Why does the notice appear once in detail, not on every step?

Repeating a "photos coming" message on every step would make it feel like a constant apology. The recipe is fully cookable — stage photos are supplementary. One calm notice at the top sets the expectation, then the step placeholders are visually quiet. In cook mode the user is focused on cooking, not on what's missing.

Why "Photos soon" not "Stage photos coming soon"?

"Stage photos" is internal language. "Photos soon" is the shortest honest label that communicates the right thing to a user: this recipe will have photos, they're not here yet. "Coming soon" was considered but reads slightly like a product launch teaser — not quite the right register. "Soon" is lighter-touch and still honest.

No placeholder on the hero image

The badge + notice are scoped to stage photos only. The hero shot will be taken for all 28 recipes as part of the hero-only shoot weekend. If a hero URL fails to load, the existing hero_fallback gradient handles it — no additional placeholder needed.
