What each direction bets on

Concept A

Search First

Bets that most users arrive knowing roughly what they want. Search is the primary action, not browsing. The featured card rewards the planned-recipe habit without forcing it.

Fewest taps to find a specific dish
Filter as a pill — no chip clutter
Consistent with CLAUDE.md: search is marquee
Risk: feels sparse if pantry + planning features aren't surfaced
Concept B

Editorial

Bets that the emotional appeal of a beautiful food publication drives engagement. The hero card is cinematic; the list layout rewards browsing by time or difficulty.

Strongest brand differentiation
Category tiles are scannable without scrolling
List rows show more metadata at a glance
Risk: hero takes a lot of vertical real estate on short Android devices
Concept C

Contextual

Bets that the right answer is personalised — not a generic library. Surfaces the planning and pantry features that make Hone different from a static recipe site.

Tonight's plan is one tap from the home screen
Pantry shortcut visible without navigating
Greeting makes the app feel like a head chef
Risk: feels empty / generic for new users with no planned recipes or pantry data
